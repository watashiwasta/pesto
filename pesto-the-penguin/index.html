Tragic Incident at Melbourne Zoo: Beloved Kangaroo Jerry Dies After Chewing on Disposable Vape

A beloved kangaroo at Melbourne Zoo has died after ingesting toxic chemicals from a discarded vape, highlighting the devastating impact of litter on wildlife.

Nicole de Souza - Staff Writer • January 2, 2024, 9:14 AEDT

SHARE THE ARTICLE

The Melbourne Zoo community is mourning the loss of one of its most beloved residents, Jerry the kangaroo, following a tragic and preventable incident. Jerry, a six-year-old eastern grey kangaroo known for his gentle demeanor and playful interactions with visitors, succumbed to complications after chewing on a disposable vape discarded in his enclosure.

The incident occurred on Tuesday afternoon when zoo staff noticed Jerry exhibiting signs of distress, including difficulty breathing and lethargy. Upon investigation, they discovered remnants of a disposable vape near Jerry's feeding area. Despite immediate veterinary intervention, Jerry passed away later that evening.

Preliminary tests suggest Jerry ingested harmful chemicals from the vape, leading to toxic shock. “This is a heartbreaking reminder of the dangers posed by littering,” said Dr. Claire Morrison, head veterinarian at Melbourne Zoo. “Disposable vapes contain substances that are incredibly harmful to wildlife. Jerry’s passing is a stark example of the consequences of human negligence.”

Visitors and staff have expressed outrage and sadness over the incident. “Jerry was a favorite among kids and families. He had such a curious nature, which unfortunately made him vulnerable,” said zoo attendant Mark Howard.

The zoo is now calling for stricter regulations on disposable vapes and urging visitors to take responsibility for their waste. “We strive to create a safe environment for our animals, but we need the public’s cooperation. Please, don’t let this happen again,” pleaded zoo director Fiona Bartlett.

Melbourne Zoo plans to install additional surveillance cameras and increase the number of staff patrolling enclosures to prevent similar tragedies. In honor of Jerry, the zoo will host an educational event next month to raise awareness about the impact of litter on wildlife.

Jerry’s story has sparked a broader conversation about the environmental dangers of disposable vapes and the importance of protecting Australia’s unique wildlife. Advocacy groups are now pushing for a national ban on the products, citing the threat they pose to animals and ecosystems.

The loss of Jerry serves as a somber reminder of the need for greater care and responsibility in preserving the lives of Australia’s cherished animals.
